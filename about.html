Data Sources

Our weather data comes from authoritative meteorological sources and historical records:

Central England Temperature (CET) Data

  • Source: Met Office Hadley Centre
  • Coverage: 1659 to present
  • Resolution: Daily mean, maximum, and minimum temperatures
  • Quality: Quality-controlled and homogenized dataset

Data Processing

All data undergoes rigorous quality control procedures including:

  • Outlier detection and removal
  • Missing data interpolation
  • Homogenization for station changes
  • Cross-validation with multiple sources

Methodology

Our analysis methods follow established climatological practices:

Statistical Analysis

We employ robust statistical methods including trend analysis, anomaly detection, and seasonal decomposition to identify patterns in weather data.

Quality Control

Multiple validation steps ensure data integrity, including cross-referencing with multiple sources and automated error detection.

Visualization Standards

Charts and graphs follow meteorological conventions with clear labeling, appropriate scales, and accessibility considerations.

Climate Context

All analyses include historical context and climate change considerations to provide meaningful interpretation of current weather patterns.

Understanding Temperature Patterns

Temperature is one of the most fundamental climate variables, and the Central England Temperature (CET) record provides an exceptional window into how temperatures have varied over time. Our analysis tools allow you to explore temperature patterns at different time scales, from daily extremes to long-term trends.

Why Central England Temperature?

The CET record is particularly valuable because:

  • Long-term perspective: Over 250 years of continuous data provides context for current climate changes
  • Geographic significance: Central England represents a temperate climate zone that's sensitive to global climate variations
  • Data quality: Carefully maintained and quality-controlled measurements
  • Climate research: Widely used by scientists to study climate change and natural variability

Interactive Features

Our visualizations include:

  • Interactive charts: Click on data points for detailed information
  • Year range filtering: Focus on specific time periods of interest
  • Trend analysis: Linear regression lines to identify long-term patterns
  • Extreme value highlighting: Automatically identify record-breaking years
  • Export capabilities: Download high-resolution images for presentations or reports

This website uses the official Central England Temperature dataset, maintained by the UK Met Office. The data represents area-averaged temperatures for central England, providing a representative sample of the region's climate.

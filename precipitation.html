Understanding Precipitation Patterns

Precipitation is a crucial climate variable that affects agriculture, water resources, and daily life. The England & Wales Precipitation (EWP) record provides valuable insights into rainfall patterns and variability over time. Our analysis tools allow you to explore precipitation patterns at different time scales, from daily extremes to long-term trends.

Why England & Wales Precipitation?

The EWP record is particularly valuable because:

  • Long-term perspective: Over 90 years of continuous data provides context for current climate changes
  • Geographic coverage: England & Wales represents a significant portion of the UK with diverse rainfall patterns
  • Data quality: Carefully maintained and quality-controlled measurements from multiple weather stations
  • Climate research: Widely used by scientists to study rainfall variability and climate change impacts

Interactive Features

Our visualizations include:

  • Interactive charts: Click on data points for detailed information
  • Year range filtering: Focus on specific time periods of interest
  • Trend analysis: Linear regression lines to identify long-term patterns
  • Extreme value highlighting: Automatically identify record-breaking years
  • Export capabilities: Download high-resolution images for presentations or reports

The EWP record shows significant day-to-day and year-to-year variability in rainfall, with some days receiving over 50mm while others remain completely dry.

üìà Seasonal Patterns

Clear seasonal patterns emerge, with autumn and winter typically being the wettest seasons, while spring and summer are generally drier.

üåç Climate Context

England & Wales precipitation patterns are influenced by Atlantic weather systems, making them sensitive to broader climate changes affecting the North Atlantic region.

üìä Data Quality

The EWP record combines data from multiple weather stations across England & Wales, providing a representative sample of regional rainfall patterns.

Data Sources & Methodology

This website uses the official England & Wales Precipitation dataset, maintained by the UK Met Office. The data represents area-averaged rainfall for England & Wales, providing a representative sample of the region's precipitation patterns.
